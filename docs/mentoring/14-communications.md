# Communications

Every club needs to talk to its members, and doing it well — consistently, professionally, and at the right time — is the difference between a club that feels organized and one that feels chaotic. The Communications module provides the infrastructure for all outbound messaging: email templates with dynamic placeholders, bulk campaigns to targeted audiences, SMS for time-sensitive notifications, delivery tracking to know what actually reached members, and opt-in management to stay compliant with data protection regulations. This is not a simple "send email" utility — it is a complete communication platform embedded within the club management system.

The foundation of everything in this module is the CommunicationTemplate entity. Templates are pre-written messages with placeholders that get filled in with real data at send time. Each club has a set of templates for common communications — a welcome email for new members, a password reset email, a payment reminder, a booking confirmation, an event notification, and a membership renewal notice. The template has a type that categorizes it, a subject line (for emails), a body with placeholder syntax, and an active flag that allows templates to be disabled without deleting them.

The placeholder syntax uses double curly braces — things like FirstName, LastName, ClubName, MemberNumber, Amount, DueDate, SessionTitle, EventTitle, ResetLink. When a communication is sent, the system resolves each placeholder by looking up the relevant data. FirstName comes from the member record. ClubName comes from the club record. Amount comes from the invoice or payment context. This resolution happens at send time, not at template creation time, which means templates always use current data.

Template customization is important because every club has its own voice. A formal cricket club like the MCC might write "Dear Mr. Smith, We write to inform you that your membership renewal is due." A casual football club might write "Hey Oliver! Your membership is up for renewal — sort it out before the deadline!" The platform provides default templates that clubs can customize to match their tone. The template editor would allow rich text formatting, though the stored format is plain text with placeholders for maximum compatibility across email clients.

Campaigns are bulk communications sent to a group of members based on targeting criteria. A club manager might want to send a "Season Starting Soon" email to all active members. Or a payment reminder to everyone with an outstanding balance over fifty pounds. Or a "We miss you" re-engagement email to members who have not attended a session in the last three months. Campaigns have several components: the target audience (defined by filters on member status, membership type, last activity date, custom fields, or manual selection), the content (either a template or custom one-off content), a scheduled send time (immediate or future), and tracking metadata.

The targeting system for campaigns is flexible and composable. You can combine multiple filters: "Active members AND membership type is Adult Full Member AND joined more than one year ago AND has not attended a session in the last 30 days." Each filter narrows the audience. The system previews the audience count before sending so the manager can verify they are reaching the right people. This prevents accidental mass emails to the wrong group — a common and embarrassing mistake in club communications.

Campaign delivery happens in batches to avoid overwhelming email providers and to allow for rate limiting. If a campaign targets three hundred members, the system does not fire three hundred emails simultaneously. It processes them in batches of fifty with a short delay between batches. This respects email provider sending limits and reduces the chance of being flagged as spam. Each individual send within a campaign is tracked independently — you can see that two hundred and ninety-two were delivered, five bounced (invalid email addresses), and three failed (temporary provider error).

Campaign analytics track engagement metrics: how many emails were sent, how many were delivered (did not bounce), how many were opened (tracked via a pixel if the club enables open tracking), and how many had links clicked (tracked via redirect URLs). These metrics help clubs understand which communications resonate with their members and which get ignored. A campaign with a two percent open rate suggests the subject line needs work. A campaign with high opens but low clicks suggests the content is not compelling enough to drive action.

SMS support is included for time-sensitive communications where email is too slow. Session cancellations need to reach members within minutes, not hours. A last-minute venue change for tonight's match needs immediate delivery. An urgent safety notice about a facility closure needs to go out right now. SMS has near-instant delivery and much higher read rates than email — most people read a text within three minutes of receiving it.

The SMS integration uses a provider abstraction similar to the payment provider pattern. An ISmsProvider interface defines methods for sending single messages and bulk messages. In production, this would be implemented with Twilio, MessageBird, or a similar SMS gateway. In development, the implementation logs what would be sent without actually sending anything. The provider abstraction means you can switch SMS providers without changing any module code — just swap the registered implementation.

SMS credits are a limited resource that ties into the subscription tier system. The Free tier might include zero SMS credits. Starter might include one hundred per month. Pro might include five hundred. Enterprise might include unlimited. The FeatureGateService checks the remaining SMS credit balance before allowing a send. If the club has exhausted their monthly allocation, the send fails with a clear error message suggesting they upgrade their plan or purchase an SMS add-on pack.

Delivery logging is comprehensive and serves multiple purposes. Every communication sent — whether email or SMS, whether individual or part of a campaign — gets a delivery log entry. The log records the recipient (member ID and contact details), the channel (email or SMS), the template or campaign it belongs to, the timestamp, the delivery status (Queued, Sent, Delivered, Bounced, Failed, Opened, Clicked), and any error messages from the provider. This gives club managers complete visibility into their communication history.

The delivery log also serves as a debugging tool. If a member says "I never received my booking confirmation," the club manager can look up the delivery log for that member and see exactly what was sent, when, and whether it was delivered or bounced. If it bounced, the error message (like "mailbox full" or "address does not exist") tells them why. This eliminates the guessing game of "did the email go out or not?"

The opt-in system respects member preferences and keeps the platform compliant with data protection regulations like GDPR. Each member has three preference flags: EmailOptIn (receive emails), SmsOptIn (receive text messages), and MarketingOptIn (receive promotional and campaign communications). These are distinct because the rules are different for each.

Transactional communications — booking confirmations, password resets, payment receipts, session cancellation notices — are always sent regardless of opt-in status. These are essential for the service to function and members have a legitimate expectation of receiving them. You cannot opt out of receiving your password reset email.

Marketing communications — campaigns, newsletters, promotional offers, re-engagement emails — respect the MarketingOptIn flag. If a member has opted out of marketing, they are excluded from campaign audiences regardless of other targeting criteria. The system enforces this at the query level — the audience filter always includes a check for MarketingOptIn being true.

The integration with other modules happens through integration events. When a session is cancelled, the Sessions module publishes a SessionCancelled event. The Communications module subscribes to this event and automatically sends cancellation notifications to all booked members using the BookingCancellation template. When a membership is about to expire, the Memberships module publishes a MembershipExpiringEvent, and Communications sends a renewal reminder. When a payment fails, the Payments module publishes a PaymentFailedEvent, and Communications sends a payment retry notification.

This event-driven approach means the Communications module does not need to know the business logic of other modules. It just knows how to send messages when told to. The triggering logic lives in the module that owns the business event. This keeps responsibilities clean and makes it easy to add new communication triggers without modifying the Communications module itself.

The seed data creates twenty-four communication templates — six per club, covering the standard template types. Each template has realistic content with appropriate placeholders for its type. The welcome template uses FirstName, ClubName, and MemberNumber. The payment reminder uses FirstName, Amount, and DueDate. This gives you working templates for testing the communication flow end-to-end.
