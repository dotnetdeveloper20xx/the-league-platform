# Programs and Courses

The Programs module handles structured learning and development activities — coaching courses, summer camps, academy programs, skills workshops, and certification pathways. This is fundamentally different from regular sessions because programs have a defined curriculum, a start and end date spanning multiple weeks or months, enrollment limits, progression tracking, and often result in some kind of certificate or qualification at the end. Think of sessions as individual classes and programs as entire courses — a program contains multiple sessions arranged in a deliberate sequence with learning objectives.

A Program entity represents the overall offering — "Junior Cricket Academy" or "Adult Beginner Hockey Course" or "Level 2 Coaching Certificate." It has a title, a detailed description explaining what participants will learn, a program type (Course, Camp, Academy, Workshop, Certification), a start date and end date defining the program's duration, a maximum enrollment capacity, a fee for the entire program, and a status lifecycle. The fee is typically paid upfront when enrolling, though payment plans could split it into installments for more expensive programs like coaching certifications that might cost several hundred pounds.

The program status lifecycle mirrors the event lifecycle in some ways but with important differences. A program starts in Draft while the club is still planning the curriculum, confirming instructors, and setting dates. It moves to Published when the details are finalized and the club wants to announce it. EnrollmentOpen allows members to actually sign up. InProgress means the program has started and sessions are being delivered. Completed means all sessions have been delivered and the program is finished. Cancelled terminates the program early.

The key difference from events is the InProgress state — a program can be in progress for weeks or months, during which individual sessions are delivered, attendance is tracked, and progression is recorded. An event's InProgress state lasts hours at most. This temporal difference drives different UI patterns — a program needs a dashboard showing progress through the curriculum, upcoming sessions, and completion percentage.

Enrollment is the equivalent of booking for programs. When a member enrolls, an Enrollment entity is created linking the member to the program. Enrollment can be waitlisted if the program is full, following the same pattern as session bookings. The enrollment fee is processed through the Payments module when enrollment is confirmed. Enrollment has its own status — Enrolled (active participant), Waitlisted (waiting for a spot), Withdrawn (left the program early), Completed (finished all requirements), and Failed (did not meet completion criteria).

The distinction between Completed and Failed enrollment statuses is important for certification programs. If a coaching course requires attending at least eighty percent of sessions and passing a practical assessment, a member who attends every session and passes gets Completed status and receives their certificate. A member who drops out halfway gets Withdrawn. A member who attends all sessions but fails the assessment gets Failed — they participated fully but did not meet the standard. Each status has different implications for refund eligibility and for whether the member can re-enroll.

Attendance tracking within programs is more granular and more important than for regular sessions. Each program has multiple scheduled sessions — think of them as classes within a course. A twelve-week coaching course has twelve sessions, one per week. Attendance is tracked per session per enrolled member. The AttendanceEntry record captures which member attended which session, their attendance status (Present, Absent, Late, Excused), and any notes from the instructor. The cumulative attendance record determines whether the member meets the completion criteria.

Progression tracking distinguishes programs from simple session series. As a member progresses through a course, their achievements can be recorded — skills mastered, levels completed, assessments passed, practical demonstrations observed. A junior cricket academy might track batting technique, bowling accuracy, fielding skills, and game awareness as separate progression areas. Each area can have levels (beginner, intermediate, advanced) and the instructor records when a participant demonstrates competency at each level.

This progression data is valuable beyond the individual program. It builds a picture of the member's development over time. A parent can see that their child has progressed from beginner to intermediate batting over two academy terms. A coach can identify which members are ready to move up to the senior squad based on their accumulated skill assessments. The data feeds into the Analytics module for understanding which programs produce the best development outcomes.

Certificate generation is the culmination of a certification program. When a member meets all completion criteria — attendance threshold met, assessments passed, practical requirements fulfilled — the system can generate a certificate. The certificate includes the member's name, the program title, the completion date, the issuing club's name, and potentially a unique verification code that can be checked online. For coaching qualifications, this certificate might be recognized by the sport's governing body.

Instructor assignment links coaches or qualified staff to programs. A program might have a lead instructor responsible for curriculum delivery and one or two assistants who help with practical sessions. The assignment records which instructor is responsible for which program, their role (Lead, Assistant, Guest), and their availability for each scheduled session. This is important for scheduling — you need to ensure the instructor is available for all the program's sessions and does not have conflicts with other commitments like match days or other programs.

The scheduling aspect of programs is more complex than sessions because you are planning multiple sessions in advance. A twelve-week course needs twelve time slots, ideally on the same day and time each week for consistency. But real life intervenes — bank holidays, facility maintenance, instructor holidays. The system needs to handle exceptions gracefully, allowing individual sessions within a program to be rescheduled or cancelled without affecting the overall program.

Communication integration is essential for programs because of their extended duration. Members need enrollment confirmations, weekly session reminders, progress updates, and completion notifications. Instructors need participant lists, attendance summaries, and alerts about members who are falling behind on attendance. Club managers need enrollment reports, revenue tracking, and completion statistics. Each of these communication needs is served by templates in the Communications module, triggered by events published from the Programs module.

The financial model for programs is typically simpler than memberships but more complex than session fees. The program has a single fee that covers all sessions. Some clubs offer early-bird discounts for enrolling before a certain date. Some offer sibling discounts when multiple family members enroll. Some offer returning-participant discounts for members who completed a previous level. These discounts are applied at enrollment time and the actual fee paid is recorded on the enrollment entity.

When you are extending this module, think about the temporal nature of the data. Programs have a past (completed sessions with attendance records and progression data), a present (the current week's session, who is attending, what is being taught), and a future (upcoming sessions that members need to be reminded about, assessments that need to be scheduled). Your queries and UI need to handle all three perspectives. The program dashboard should show where we are in the curriculum, who is on track for completion, and what is coming next. Historical views should show completed programs with their outcomes. Planning views should show upcoming programs with their enrollment status.
